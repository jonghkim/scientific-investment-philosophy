# Investment Strategies in FinTech

This repository contains teaching materials for the guest lecture
**"Investment Strategies in FinTech"**, delivered in the
KAIST FMBA course *Introduction to FinTech* (Prof. Byungtae Lee).

---

## Teaching Summary

- **Instructor:** Jongho Kim (Ph.D. Candidate, Cornell University)
- **Audience:** MBA / Executive MBA students (KAIST FMBA)
- **Level:** Applied quantitative finance / FinTech

This lecture introduces the economic and statistical foundations of modern
fintech investment platforms, with a focus on:

1. **Robo-advisory systems**
   - Portfolio construction under risk preferences
   - Role of ETFs and passive investing
   - Behavioral interventions (nudging)

2. **Empirical asset pricing models**
   - CAPM and factor models
   - Decomposition of returns (alpha vs. factor exposure)
   - Evidence on active vs. passive performance

The materials emphasize a **data-driven and empirical approach** to
investment strategies, connecting financial theory with real-world
implementation in digital platforms.
